Research Interests
  • 1. Field Emission Point Electron Source Research and Applications
  • High time-space resolution/spin polarization/cryoelectron microscope, X-ray tube, chip multi-beam detection/processing equipment, scanning tunneling microscope, low energy electron diffraction, etc.
  • 2. In-situ SEM/TEM Study on Electrical and Thermal Properties of Nanomaterials
  • Graphene, carbon nanotubes, nanowires, nanocones, two-dimensional nanosheets, etc.
  • 3. Preparation, Characterization and Application of Low-dimensional Nanomaterials
  • Bottom-up: CVD growth; Top-down: preparation of FIB fine structure
  • We are committed to developing point electron sources with higher resolution, more Qualcomm and more stable emission for high-end electron microscopes and chip inspection/processing equipment.
  • We are also committed to exploring the electrical properties of materials at nanometer and atomic scales in situ by high resolution electron microscopy.
  • Our research attaches great importance to international cooperation and exchanges, and currently has good cooperative relations with top universities and research institutions such as National Institute of Materials (NIMS) of Japan and University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (UNC) of the United States.
